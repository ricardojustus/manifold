# Encode on repetition — the third time, propose structure

**Rule.** The **~third time** the operator asks for — or you perform — the same multi-step procedure, **propose encoding it**: a skill for a procedure, a rule for a constraint, a template for an artifact shape. Propose; do not unilaterally create. Repeated detailed asks are the signal that judgment has stabilized enough to become structure.

## Why

A procedure re-explained every time is a tax the operator pays repeatedly and a place the agent re-derives (and occasionally gets wrong) every time. The operator experiences this directly as "things I have to ask for in detail, over and over." The first time is novel; the second might be coincidence; by the third, the shape is stable and the pattern is worth capturing — later than that and you're just paying the tax longer. Encoding it once converts a recurring cost into a fixed one and makes the procedure *consistent* instead of re-improvised.

The reflex has a natural counterpart: the retrospective mining pass catches what the live reflex misses (scan recent work for repeated detailed asks). But the live reflex is cheaper — catching the repetition *as it happens* beats finding it in an audit weeks later.

Two guardrails keep this from over-firing. **Propose, don't unilaterally create** — the operator decides whether a pattern is worth freezing; a skill or rule they didn't sanction is clutter they now have to maintain. And **encode the right kind of thing**: a *procedure* (multi-step how-to) becomes a skill; a *constraint* (an always-true "don't do X") becomes a rule; an *artifact shape* (a document structure reused across instances) becomes a template. Miscategorizing — a one-shot procedure frozen as an always-loaded rule, a reflexive constraint buried in an on-demand skill — costs context budget and misfires.

*Receipt: an operator found themselves specifying the same multi-step dispatch procedure in detail on each new run, experiencing it as "tasks I have to re-explain every time." The fix was not a bigger explanation — it was noticing the repetition on the third pass and proposing the procedure become a reusable skill, so the next ask was one word instead of a paragraph.*

## How to apply

- Keep a light count: when a procedure or constraint recurs a third time, that's the trigger.
- Classify before proposing: procedure → skill, constraint → rule, artifact shape → template. Name which and why.
- **Propose** it to the operator with the classification and a one-line rationale; let them approve. Don't spawn the artifact on your own authority.
- If a live reflex is missed, the retro/consistency pass is the backstop — but bias toward catching it live.
